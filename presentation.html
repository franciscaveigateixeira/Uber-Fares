Data Preparation

From 4 Raw Columns → 32 Features

First, I cleaned the data: removed invalid GPS coordinates, negative fares, and impossible distances.

Then I created new features through engineering:

  • distance_km — Haversine formula from GPS coords
  • pickup_hour — extracted from timestamp
  • fare_per_km — fare ÷ distance
  • day_of_week — extracted from timestamp

K-Means

Groups 191K trips into k clusters by minimizing distance to each group's center. Unsupervised — no labels needed.

Features used: distance_km, pickup_hour, fare_per_km, passenger_count

PCA

Compresses 4 features into 2 dimensions so we can visualize clusters in a 2D scatter plot. Preserves maximum variance.

Used for visualization only — K-Means runs on all 4 features.

Choosing the Right K

How I Chose k = 8

Elbow Method

Plotted WCSS (within-cluster sum of squares) for k = 2 to 10. The curve bends — or "elbows" — at k = 8, meaning adding more clusters gives diminishing returns.

Silhouette Score

Measures how well-separated the clusters are (–1 to 1). My score: 0.39 — considered good separation for real-world data at this scale.

optimal clusters

What the Results Mean

Business Insights from the Data

Strategy 1
Profitability Peak

Highest fare/km happens at 4–6 AM, not at rush hour. Low volume but maximum margin per km.

→ Incentivize drivers in early morning
Strategy 2
Fleet Waste

69% of all trips have just 1 passenger — using 4-seat cars means 75% of capacity is empty.

→ Invest in compact EVs + Uber Pool
Strategy 3
Volume vs Premium

Short-distance clusters = high volume → focus retention budget here. Long-distance clusters = premium, no discounts.

→ Segment-based marketing
Most Interesting Finding

The 4 AM Paradox

At first this seemed wrong:

The Explanation

The K-Means "Long Night Ride" cluster revealed it: almost ALL 4 AM trips are airport transfers — long, urgent, no alternatives. Passengers have no choice but to pay.

This was invisible in raw data — only visible when crossing hour, distance, and fare/km through the clusters.
